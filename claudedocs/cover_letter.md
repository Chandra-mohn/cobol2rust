Dear [CXO Name],

I am writing to propose a strategic partnership between [Company Name] and a
new venture I am founding, focused on building enterprise-grade developer
tools for legacy modernization, architecture management, and deterministic
code generation.

Over my tenure at [Company Name], I have had the privilege of working closely
with engineering teams navigating the challenges that every large enterprise
faces: aging COBOL systems that resist change, stream processing pipelines
that demand deep expertise, data migrations that carry unacceptable risk, and
architecture complexity that slows innovation.

These experiences led me to build Nexflow -- a platform your leadership team
has seen firsthand and recognized for its ability to eliminate hand-coded
Flink jobs entirely through deterministic DSL-to-code generation. The
response to Nexflow confirmed what I had long believed: there is a
significant gap in the market for tools that transform enterprise engineering
through precision and determinism, not probabilistic AI guesswork.

Nexflow is one of six products I have built, each addressing a distinct stage
of the enterprise modernization lifecycle:

- **Nexflow** -- Deterministic stream processing code generation (DSL to
  production Apache Flink)
- **cobol2rust** -- Automated COBOL-to-Rust transpilation with full runtime
  support (901 tests passing, 46/47 stress tests validated)
- **coqu** -- Interactive COBOL analysis and query tool for legacy code
  comprehension
- **ArchStudio** -- Cloud architecture visualization, modeling, and Terraform
  integration
- **DVF** -- Multi-source data validation framework for migration integrity
  assurance
- And additional products in development along the same line

Together, these products form a unified platform that takes enterprises from
legacy understanding through modernization to validated, production-ready
modern systems.

**What I am proposing is not a departure -- it is a deepening of alignment.**

I would like [Company Name] to be the founding strategic partner of this
venture. Specifically, I am proposing:

1. **Preferred Licensing** -- [Company Name] receives enterprise licensing at
   founding-partner rates, well below market pricing
2. **Co-Development Influence** -- A seat at the product advisory table,
   ensuring the roadmap serves [Company Name]'s real-world needs
3. **Equity Participation** -- An optional minority equity stake, giving
   [Company Name] financial upside as the platform scales to other
   enterprises
4. **Continuity of Value** -- Rather than losing institutional knowledge when
   talent moves on, [Company Name] gains a dedicated technology partner whose
   tools are battle-tested against your exact challenges

I recognize this is an unconventional ask. But the alternative -- building
these tools inside a single company's IT department -- limits their impact
and denies [Company Name] the strategic advantage of being the founding
partner of a platform that serves an industry.

The enclosed presentation details the product portfolio, market opportunity,
partnership model, and financial framework. I welcome the opportunity to
present this in person and discuss how we can structure a partnership that
creates lasting value for both sides.

Thank you for your time and consideration.
