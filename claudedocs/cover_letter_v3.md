Dear [CXO Name],

I am writing to propose a strategic partnership built on three ideas:

**Modernization** -- legacy systems can be transformed into modern,
performant code with engineering precision, not consulting guesswork.

**Domain-Specific Languages** -- business rules belong in a language
that domain experts can read and compilers can execute, not buried in
boilerplate that only the original developer understands.

**AI as the Accelerator** -- AI should propose solutions, not ship
them. When AI drafts and humans decide, the result is faster than
manual work and more reliable than AI alone.

These three ideas form the foundation of every product I have built --
including Nexflow, which your leadership team has seen and endorsed.
Nexflow proved that this model works in production: AI drafts DSL from
business documents, domain experts review in their own language, and a
deterministic compiler generates identical production code every time.

Now I am ready to apply this model beyond stream processing -- into
APIs, microservices, NACHA payment processing, banking transactions,
and every domain where enterprises write repetitive, pattern-driven
code. Alongside this, I have built supporting tools: a COBOL-to-Rust
transpiler with 901 passing tests, a code intelligence platform for
legacy analysis, an architecture modeling system, and a data validation
framework. Each reinforces the three pillars.

I am proposing that [Company Name] become the founding strategic
partner of this venture -- with preferred licensing, product advisory
influence, optional equity participation, and the opportunity to
co-create the domain DSLs that encode your institutional knowledge
into reusable, auditable products.

The enclosed 10-slide presentation details the thesis, the proof,
and the partnership model. I welcome the opportunity to present it
in person.
